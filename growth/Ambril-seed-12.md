# Ambril

*heat shimmer, boomtowns, the chord you hear in your teeth*

> The Quiet Restoration · 1096 AF · about ~1660s Earth.
> **the old dynasty was put back on a throne it no longer fits, 40 years ago.**
> It is heading toward a war everyone can see and nobody will name.

Entries marked ★ do not come from the seed. They exist because of how one woman
spent her life here — the world grew where she pressed on it, and nowhere else.

## How it works

**Technology.** Tier 5 — bronze, and very good at it. It runs on godash — refined divine remains.
Also: no electricity — the world skipped it entirely.
Also: writing is common; reading is a licensed profession.

**Magic.** Ubiquitous — people catch it like weather. It comes from debt. someone owed you, and it has to come out somewhere.

- **Emberment** — inherited and involuntary; makes a thing what it is called. *patriotic.*
  **Cost: it takes your capacity to be surprised, which you will miss.**
- **Emberwork** — drawn on the body; preserves — nothing rots inside it. *outlawed, ubiquitous.*
  **Cost: the price rises every year and nobody can find who is setting it.**
- **Saltment** — sung, badly, on purpose; heat, force, industry. *patriotic.*
  **Cost: you lose the ability to recognise faces, starting with the ones you love.**

**Limits:** it cannot lie, so it cannot be used to deceive, so it is used to interrogate.

**Legally:** legal, taxed at 40%, and the tax is the only thing anyone complies with.

**The gods.** They were people, and the paperwork survives. They interact silent for eight hundred years, and ascension is possible, and three have done it, and all three regret it.

- **The Quiet** — *silent.* Has never spoken, intervened, or asked for anything.
  This frightens theologians more than the hostile one does.
- **Vell** — *alive, attentive.* Keeps accounts. Does not punish — records, then makes the record true.
  Broken oaths fade your name from documents, then from mouths.
- **Orrun** — *ascended, recently, and it went badly.* Was a man. There are people alive who owed him money.
  The paperwork of his mortal life is a live theological crisis.

**The money.** water → nothing — water does not leave, paid in water-roll credits.
The money sits with whoever wrote the roll. **The bill is paid by everyone struck off it.**
